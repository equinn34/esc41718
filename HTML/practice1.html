Harriet Tubman was an African-American woman.

She escaped slavery right before the Civil War, and became a leading abolitionist.

She risked her life to lead hundres of slaves to freedom.

Picture of Harriet Tubman

  • She made a daring escape from slavery when she was in her twenties.
  • Harriet Tubman guided at least 70 slaves to freedom.
  • She was a prominent voice in the campaign for women's suffrage.
  • She helped in the establishment of The Harriet Tubman Home for the Aged.
  • She was the first African-American woman to be honored on a US Postage stamp.
